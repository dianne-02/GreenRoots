The Global Threat: Rampant Deforestation

Our planet's forestsâ€”vital lungs of the Earthâ€”are disappearing at an alarming rate, threatening biodiversity, climate stability, and human well-being.

Loss Rate: The world loses an area of forest roughly equivalent to the size of 30 football fields every minute. This massive loss is driven by agricultural expansion, unsustainable logging, and urbanization.

Climate Impact: Deforestation contributes to approximately 11% of global greenhouse gas emissions. Trees are essential carbon sinks; when they are cut down, the stored carbon is released into the atmosphere.

Biodiversity Crisis: Forests harbor over 80% of the world's terrestrial species. Their destruction is the leading cause of species extinction and habitat fragmentation globally.
